Members of our Prague group participated in the [Gothenburg CHAIR Workshop 2026](https://josefurban.eu/chalmers_workshop_webpage.html) (May 26–29, 2026) at Chalmers University of Technology, Gothenburg, Sweden. The workshop brought together researchers working on AI-guided automated and interactive theorem proving.

We presented three talks covering our recent work:

+ **[Martin Suda](https://people.ciirc.cvut.cz/~sudamar2/)** presented *"Strong Within Benchmarks, Weak Across Them: Evaluating Neural Guidance in Vampire"*, examining how learned guidance in the Vampire ATP system generalises across different problem benchmarks.

+ **[Jan Jakubův](https://people.ciirc.cvut.cz/~jakubja5/)** presented *"Learning-Guided Higher-Order Automated Reasoning for Isabelle/HOL"*, showcasing our progress on integrating machine learning into higher-order automated reasoning within the Isabelle/HOL ecosystem.

+ **[Keneni W. Tesema](https://usermap.cvut.cz/profile/1d88ef8a-5f02-4efb-964c-7a10a4ee4500)** presented *"Premise Selection for Higher-Order ATP Problems"*, addressing the challenge of selecting relevant premises for higher-order automated theorem proving.
